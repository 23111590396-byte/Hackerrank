Visa — Travel Services and International Support

## Traveling with Visa

### Using Visa internationally
Visa is accepted in over 200 countries and territories worldwide, making it one of the most widely accepted payment methods. Look for the Visa acceptance symbol at merchants, ATMs, and payment terminals.

### Before you travel

**Notify your bank**: Inform your bank of your travel dates and destinations before you leave. This prevents your card from being blocked due to unusual geographic activity. Most banks allow notification via mobile app, online banking, or phone.

**Check card expiry**: Ensure your card won't expire during your trip. Request a replacement card before departing if it expires soon.

**Have a backup**: Carry more than one card (if possible) and keep emergency contacts for your bank. Write down the international collect number on the back of your card separately from the card itself.

**Enable international transactions**: Some cards require you to enable international or online transactions specifically. Check with your bank.

### ATMs abroad
Visa-branded ATMs worldwide accept Visa debit and credit cards for cash withdrawals. Look for the Visa or Plus network logo on ATMs. Fees typically include:
- ATM owner fee (paid to the local bank)
- Foreign transaction fee (charged by your bank, typically 1-3%)
- Currency conversion (if converting to local currency)

To minimize fees: use ATMs affiliated with major banks, withdraw larger amounts less frequently, and check if your bank has international ATM partnerships.

### Currency exchange at ATMs vs. currency exchange offices
ATMs generally offer better exchange rates than currency exchange offices at airports or hotels. When an ATM asks if you want to "accept the exchange rate" (Dynamic Currency Conversion/DCC), choose to pay in local currency — DCC rates are typically unfavorable.

### Dynamic Currency Conversion (DCC)
DCC is when a merchant or ATM offers to convert the transaction to your home currency at the point of sale. While convenient, DCC rates are typically 3-7% worse than Visa's exchange rate. Always choose to pay in local currency when given the option.

## Emergency Services Abroad

### Lost or stolen card while traveling
1. **Immediately call your bank** at the international number listed on the back of your card (call collect if needed).
2. **Visa Global Customer Service**: +1-303-967-1096 (available 24/7, collect)
3. Your bank will block the card immediately to prevent further unauthorized use.
4. Request emergency card replacement or emergency cash disbursement.

### Emergency card replacement
Visa's emergency card replacement service can deliver a replacement card within 1-3 business days in most locations worldwide. This service is typically available for Visa Platinum and above. Contact your issuing bank to initiate emergency replacement.

### Emergency cash disbursement
If you need cash urgently and cannot wait for card replacement, Visa's emergency cash disbursement service can arrange cash through a local Visa member bank. Fees apply. Contact your bank or Visa Global Customer Service.

### Medical emergencies abroad
Some Visa cards include travel medical insurance. Check your cardholder benefits guide or contact your bank. Visa Signature and Visa Infinite cards often include significant travel insurance. Visa Assistance Center (for eligible cardholders): 1-800-992-6029 (US) or collect +1-303-967-1093.

## Visa Travel Insurance Benefits

### Types of travel insurance on Visa cards
Benefits vary by card tier and issuing bank. Common Visa travel benefits include:
- **Trip cancellation/interruption insurance**: Reimburses prepaid non-refundable travel costs for covered reasons.
- **Travel accident insurance**: Coverage for accidents during travel.
- **Baggage delay insurance**: Compensation if baggage is delayed more than 12 hours.
- **Lost luggage reimbursement**: Reimbursement for lost/stolen checked baggage.
- **Emergency evacuation coverage**: Medical evacuation in life-threatening situations (Visa Infinite tier).
- **Auto rental collision damage waiver**: Covers rental car damage (when you pay with your Visa card and decline the rental company's CDW).

### How to make a travel insurance claim
Contact the benefits administrator listed in your cardholder guide (varies by bank and card). You typically need: your Visa card, documentation of the covered event, receipts for expenses claimed. File claims promptly — most have a deadline of 60-90 days from the event.

### Auto rental CDW (Collision Damage Waiver)
When you pay for a rental car with your eligible Visa card and decline the rental company's CDW, Visa may cover damage to the rental vehicle. Coverage is secondary to your personal auto insurance in the US and primary in many international locations. Read your benefits guide for limitations (luxury cars, trucks, and motorcycles may be excluded).

## International Merchant Acceptance

### What if a merchant won't accept my Visa card?
In rare cases, merchants may decline Visa cards or prefer local payment methods. If a Visa card is refused at a merchant that displays the Visa acceptance mark, ask the merchant to retry the transaction. If still refused, contact your bank to check if the transaction was blocked on their end. You can also report acceptance issues to Visa at visa.com/merchantfeedback.

### Visa acceptance in specific regions
Visa is widely accepted in Europe, North America, Australia, and most of Asia. In some regions (cash-dominant economies), card acceptance may be limited outside major cities and tourist areas. Research local payment customs before traveling to developing regions.
